Mental Health

Reasons to take care of your mental health. 

Overall well-being

Just like physical health, mental health is a crucial component of your overall well-being. It affects how you think, feel, and act. When your mental health is good, you're better able to handle stress, cope with challenges, and build strong relationships.

Physical health

There's a strong connection between mental and physical health. Poor mental health can increase your risk of physical health problems, and vice versa. Taking care of your mental health can help you stay physically healthy as well.

Relationships

Good mental health allows you to build and maintain healthy relationships with others. It helps you communicate effectively, manage conflict, and feel empathy for others.

Positive self-image

Mental health plays a big role in how you see yourself. When your mental health is good, you're more likely to have a positive self-image and healthy self-esteem.

Resilience

Taking care of your mental health can help you develop resilience, the ability to bounce back from difficult times. This is important because life inevitably throws challenges our way, and mental strength helps you navigate them.

Productivity and enjoyment of life

When your mental health is good, you're better able to focus, be productive, and achieve your goals. You're also more likely to enjoy the activities you do and experience a greater sense of satisfaction in life.

MoCA Test

Montreal Cognitive Assessment

  • Early detection
  • Quick assessment
  • Broad evaluation
  • Improved diagnosis
  • Risk identification
  • Differential screening
  • Treatment planning

It's a 30-point screening tool that assesses cognitive impairment in older adults and can detect early signs of dementia. The MoCA test takes about 10 minutes to complete and assesses eight cognitive domains, including: Attention, Executive function, Language, Memory, Naming, Orientation, Abstraction, and Visuospatial function.

Predict Now

Detecting Alzheimer's disease early is critical. It allows patients to access medications that slow the disease's progress and plan for their future care while they still can. Early diagnosis also opens doors to potentially beneficial clinical trials and support networks, improving the patient's quality of life. This clarity can significantly reduce anxiety for both the patient and their loved ones. Furthermore, early detection can lead to cost savings in healthcare by enabling proactive management of the disease.
